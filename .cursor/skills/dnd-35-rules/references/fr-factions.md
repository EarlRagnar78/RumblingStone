# Forgotten Realms — Factions & Organizations (D&D 3.5)

Sources: FRCS (2001), Lords of Darkness (2003), Power of Faerûn (2006), various sourcebooks

---

## THE HARPERS

**Alignment**: CG / NG (varies by member)
**Goals**: Preserve the balance of power across Faerûn; oppose tyranny and the rise of evil
power-blocs; protect individual freedom; foster the spread of knowledge
**Founded**: ~440 DR by a bard named Elminster (actually: Moon Lady Miriimele and various
bards in the Delimbiyr Vale); formally organized ~1021 DR
**Headquarters**: Hidden safehouses across Faerûn; no central HQ; the Twilight Hall in Berdusk
is a major known base. The High Harper council meets rarely.
**Symbol**: Crescent moon + harp (silver/white)
**Structure**: Highly decentralized cell structure. Members rarely know other members outside their
cell. Three ranks: Harper, Harper Agent (Harper Scout PrC), High Harper.
**Notable Members**: Elminster Aumar (sponsor/advisor), Khelben Arunsun (estranged), the Seven
Sisters (loosely affiliated), Dove Falconhand, Storm Silverhand, Caledan Caldorien
**Magic**: Harpers carry the Harper Pin (minor magic item that identifies them to other Harpers).
**Status (1372 DR)**: Active but fractured. Khelben Arunsun broke away to form the Moonstars.
Some members feel the organization has become too interventionist; others too passive.
**Enemies**: Zhentarim (primary), Red Wizards of Thay, Cult of the Dragon, Shades

## THE ZHENTARIM (The Black Network)

**Alignment**: LE (officially); LE/NE (most members)
**Goals**: Economic and political domination of all Faerûn trade routes; expand power of the
Black Network; serve Bane (officially since 1372 DR following his return)
**Founded**: ~1261 DR by Manshoon and Fzoul Chembryl
**Headquarters**: Darkhold (stronghold in Sunset Mountains); Zhentil Keep (rebuilding as of 1372);
Castle Zhentil; numerous hidden fortresses
**Symbol**: Black hand (Bane's symbol) in many contexts; they often use no symbol
**Structure**: Two power blocs in constant tension — the Mage Arm (Manshoon's clones, arcane
faction) and the Church Arm (Fzoul Chembryl's Banite priesthood).
Fzoul Chembryl: High Imperceptor of Bane, de facto ruler of the Black Network in 1372 DR.
Manshoon: Original founder; his clone-war scattered versions of him across Faerûn.
**Notable Members**: Fzoul Chembryl, Manshoon (multiple clones), Sememmon (Darkhold commander),
Ashemmi (Sememmon's companion), Pereghost (undead knight commander)
**Operations**: Control of the Black Road (overland trade route). Mercenary contracts.
Caravans through Zhentilar soldiers. Slave trade. Drug smuggling. Political assassination.
**Status (1372 DR)**: Rebuilding after the destruction of Zhentil Keep (1368 DR). Bane's
return has energized the Banite clergy. Expanding aggressively.
**Resources**: Largest private army in Faerûn. Extensive spy network. Deep financial reserves.

## THE RED WIZARDS OF THAY

**Alignment**: NE (most); LE (organized structure)
**Goals**: Dominate Faerûn magically; accumulate personal power; advance the interests of
Thay as a nation; individual Zulkirs have personal agendas
**Founded**: As a nation ~922 DR; Red Wizard title codified from founding
**Headquarters**: Eltabbar (capital of Thay); the eight Zulkirs maintain individual fortresses
**Symbol**: Red robes; tattooed shaved heads; no universal symbol but associated with red
**Structure**: The Council of Zulkirs (8 archmages, one per school) nominally governs.
In practice, Szass Tam (lich, Necromancy Zulkir) holds disproportionate power.
The eight Zulkirs: Szass Tam (Necromancy), Nevron (Conjuration), Lauzoril (Enchantment),
Dmitra Flass (Illusion), Aznar Thrul (Evocation), Yaphyll (Divination), Invarri Metron (Abjuration),
Druxus Rhym (Transmutation)
**Operations**: Red Wizard Enclaves throughout Faerûn — shops where magic items are sold at below-
market prices (an investment to gain economic footholds). Slave trade. Political manipulation.
**Notable Members**: Szass Tam (most powerful lich in Faerûn), Tharchions (governors), various
individual Red Wizard PCs/NPCs
**Status (1372 DR)**: Powerful and expanding. Enclaves are multiplying. Szass Tam is plotting
something larger than typical political maneuvering. Annex of Unther gives new territory.
**Enemies**: Harpers, Aglarond (The Simbul in particular), various good-aligned powers

## THE LORDS' ALLIANCE

**Alignment**: LN (official); member cities range from LG to N
**Goals**: Mutual defense against common threats; free trade between member cities; oppose the
rise of tyranny across the Sword Coast and North
**Members**: Waterdeep (dominant), Baldur's Gate, Silverymoon, Neverwinter, Berdusk, Scornubel,
Elturel, Everlund, Sundabar, Mirabar, Secomber, Yartar, Triboar, Longsaddle
**Headquarters**: No fixed HQ; major meetings in Waterdeep
**Symbol**: Varies; often a tower or city-state seal
**Notable Members**: Lords of Waterdeep (primary drivers), Lord Nasher of Neverwinter, Alustriel
of Silverymoon, Grand Duke Entar Silvershield (Baldur's Gate), various Open Lords
**Status (1372 DR)**: Active. The primary legitimate counter to the Zhentarim in the North.

## THE EMERALD ENCLAVE

**Alignment**: N (neutral, balanced)
**Goals**: Protect the balance of civilization and nature; prevent either from destroying the other;
maintain the equilibrium so that both can exist
**Headquarters**: The Deepwild (druidic grove in the Western Heartlands near Turmish)
**Symbol**: A green oak leaf on a brown circle
**Structure**: Hierarchical druid circles. Nine levels: Deepwarden (lowest) to Master of the Wild
(highest). Senior members can only communicate with those within 2 ranks of them.
**Notable Members**: Silvanus clergy often affiliated; rangers, druids. The Hierophant Druid of the
Deepwild is the most powerful.
**Operations**: Protect threatened ecosystems; purge unnatural undead and aberrations from
wilderness areas; maintain forests and wild places
**Status (1372 DR)**: Quiet but powerful. Silvanus worshippers and independent druids often ally.

## THE CULT OF THE DRAGON

**Alignment**: NE
**Goals**: Transform Faerûn's most powerful dragons into liches (dracoliches), who will then rule
the world under the Cult's guidance. Based on Sammaster's prophecy: "Dead dragons shall rule
the living and stone shall serve flesh"
**Founded**: ~900 DR by Sammaster (former Chosen of Mystra who went insane)
**Headquarters**: Hidden strongholds; primary known base in the Moonsea North region
**Symbol**: A dragon skull
**Structure**: Wearers of Purple (clergy), Wearers of Black (fighters/rogues), arcane wing
**Notable Members**: Various Wearers of Purple (unnamed); Naergoth Bladelord (undead leader in some
lore); Severin (a major cultist in some novels)
**Operations**: Seek out powerful dragons; lure or coerce them into becoming dracoliches;
maintain and serve the dracolich "allies" (who are technically free but manipulated)
**Status (1372 DR)**: Active and dangerous. Several dracoliches exist in Faerûn under their
"protection." The Harpers and Red Wizards both consider them a threat.

---

## THE MOONSTARS (Telkiira)

**Alignment**: NG/LG
**Goals**: Harper-like goals but more willing to use aggressive tactics; protect Faerûn from
Netherese Shadovar (primary mission in 1372 DR)
**Founded**: ~1369 DR by Khelben "Blackstaff" Arunsun after breaking with the Harpers
**Headquarters**: Blackstaff Tower, Waterdeep
**Symbol**: A silver star (telkiira rune)
**Notable Members**: Khelben Arunsun, Laeral Silverhand, various Waterdhavian mages
**Status (1372 DR)**: Growing faction; focused on the Shadovar threat that most others underestimate

---

## BREGAN D'AERTHE

**Alignment**: CE (collectively); CN (Jarlaxle personally)
**Goals**: Profit above all. Information brokering. Mercenary contracts. Jarlaxle's personal
entertainment and the acquisition of interesting items and experiences.
**Founded**: by Jarlaxle (male drow, former Baenre agent, now freelance)
**Headquarters**: Menzoberranzan (primary); has operatives on the surface (disguised)
**Symbol**: None publicly; internal recognition signs
**Notable Members**: Jarlaxle (charismatic leader, profligate user of wands and hats),
Arathis (second), various drow male mercenaries (many escaped noble houses)
**Operations**: Intelligence services, assassination, escorting merchants through the Underdark,
surface infiltration operations
**Status (1372 DR)**: Thriving. Jarlaxle is one of the most interesting and unpredictable
power brokers in Faerûn. Has surface operations posing as merchants.

## THE ORDER OF THE GAUNTLET (Historical Precursor)

Not fully organized in 1372 DR; exists as informal Torm/Helm/Tyr worshipper fellowships.
Formal Order of the Gauntlet as an organization is a later development. In 1372: treat as
informal clerical militant orders within each deity's church.

## CITY-SPECIFIC FACTIONS

### Force Grey / The Grey Hands (Waterdeep)
Purpose: Elite agents of the Lords of Waterdeep; recruited from exceptional adventurers
Alignment: LN/LG (required)
Notable: Piergeiron the Paladinson oversees them. Very high powered (can include level 15+ members).

### The Shadow Thieves (Amn)
Alignment: NE/LE | Headquarters: Athkatla
Goals: Control all organized crime across Faerûn's western coast
Operations: Assassination guild, fencing, information brokering
Status: Expelled from Waterdeep in 1358 DR; now dominant in Amn

### The Fire Knives (Cormyr)
Alignment: LE | Headquarters: Various hidden (once Westgate)
Purpose: Assassin's guild exiled from Cormyr; revenge against the royal family
Status: Scattered but active

### The Knights of the Shield (Sword Coast)
Alignment: NE | Headquarters: Hidden council chambers in major port cities
Purpose: Shadow economic manipulation; using a yuan-ti beholder-kin god-eye as oracle
Operations: Controlling trade through information and assassination of key economic players

### The Iron Throne (Baldur's Gate environs)
Alignment: LE | Purpose: Weapon and commodity smuggling; monopoly enforcement
Status: Fractured after events of Baldur's Gate (BG video game); remnants persist

### The Many-Starred Cloak (Silverymoon)
Alignment: NG | Purpose: Protection of Silverymoon; akin to Waterdeep's Force Grey
Members: High-level mages and warriors

### The Zhentarim's Banites vs. Mage Arm
Internal Zhentarim schism. The Banite church (Fzoul) vs. Manshoon's arcane wing has been
ongoing since the network's founding and creates constant internal intrigue opportunities.

## CRIMINAL AND UNDERGROUND ORGANIZATIONS

### The Guild (various city guilds of thieves)
Most major cities have a thieves' guild. Structure and alignment vary widely.
Common names: "The Guild" (Waterdeep), "The Shadow Court" (various), "The Black Masks" (Sembia)

### Slavers' Rings
Several active slaving operations across Faerûn:
- Amn: Technically legal (with regulation); well-organized
- Calimshan: Ancient tradition; extensive slave markets in Calimport
- The Underdark: Virtually all Underdark races use slaves extensively

## MILITARY AND KNIGHTLY ORDERS

### The Purple Dragons (Cormyr)
Royal army of Cormyr; commanded by King's Officers. Elite Purple Dragon Knights are
prestige class graduates. The War Wizards support them with battle magic.

### The Flaming Fist (Baldur's Gate)
Mercenary company that effectively governs Baldur's Gate's security.
Notable: Grand Dukes use them as police force. Can be hired externally.

### The Griffon Cavalry (Waterdeep)
Elite aerial cavalry; mounts griffons bred in the Griffon's Nest barracks.

### The Moonshae Riders (Moonshae Isles)
Ffolk cavalry tradition; work alongside druids and rangers.

### The Silver Swords (Silverymoon)
Elite infantry of Silverymoon; trained by Alustriel's War Mages.

### The Riders of Nesmé (Silver Marches)
Cavalry defending Nesmé against trolls and other threats from the Evermoors.

---

## ARCANE ORGANIZATIONS

### The Arcane Brotherhood (Luskan)
Alignment: N/NE | Headquarters: Host Tower of the Arcane
Goals: Arcane power; domination of northern trade; research into old magic
Structure: Five Overwizards + High Captain allies + Archmage Arcane at top
Notable: Historically antagonistic to Silverymoon and the Harpers.

### The Order of the Mystic Fire (Mulhorand)
Divine-arcane hybrid institution serving Thoth; maintains the most complete libraries east of Faerûn.

### The Watchful Order of Magists and Protectors (Waterdeep)
Effectively the mages' guild of Waterdeep. Members pay dues and receive legal protections.
Enforces the city's arcane use regulations.

### The Halruaan Mages (no formal name — all Halruaan mages)
A society where ALL powerful people are mages. No formal organization needed — Halruaa itself
is the organization.

## CAMPAIGN-SPECIFIC FACTIONS (RumblingStone)

These factions are unique to the RumblingStone campaign and do not appear in published sourcebooks.

### The Drow Experimental Faction (Underdark)

**Alignment**: CE/NE
**Leaders**:
- **Sonjak** — Drow Cleric Matrona of Lolth. Obsessed with mixing and creating new forms of
  aberrations. Her current research focus is on **fungal aberrations**: hybridizing myconid
  creatures with drow fleshcrafting. Has made a **pact with the Mother of Fungi** (a daemon
  aberration) in Lolth's name, creating an unholy alliance between drow religious authority
  and demonic fungal corruption.
- **Il Collezionista** — A **Rakshasa** that delights in turning living enemies into macabre
  statues (using a bonded basilisk) and **selling them to evil villains** as trophies and
  decorations. Escaped from the minotaur lair encounter in the Underdark. **Recurring villain**
  who has not yet been caught or killed.

**Operations**:
- Modified Neverlight Grove into a drow research lab (tower fungi infested with web spiders,
  drow-modified creatures, and specialized aberration researchers)
- Created portal networks connecting underground experiments to surface operations
- Lurk during night phases of major battles to exploit chaos (e.g., Night of the Drow at
  Battle of Rethmar — sabotage temples to retrieve powerful evil artifacts)
- Use orcs and hobgoblins as disposable troops (standard drow proxy warfare)

**Campaign Connections**:
- Linked to arcs: Minotaur Lair (Pre-Arc 01), `02_scaladossa-abbattor-funghi`, `03_la Cittadella`
- Reappear at Battle of Rethmar (arc 09, P3 phases) as a third faction attacking during the siege
- Il Collezionista and Sonjak are connected but the party has NOT yet discovered the
  relationship between the Mother of Fungi and the drow architect Sonjak
- **Il Collezionista's evil artifact trade:** After escaping the Minotaur Lair, Il Collezionista
  formed an alliance with Sonjak's drow guild, providing **strange and pure evil artifacts** —
  possibly the same artifacts the drow seek during the Notte dei Drow temple assault at Rethmar
- **Expedition to the Demonweb Pits** (p.67) creatures used by the drow researchers in the
  fungi tower experiments
- **NPC Therysol** — Originally a slave sold by drow traders affiliated with Il Collezionista's
  guild to salamanders in the Fire Elemental Plane. After helping the party in the Fire Node
  (Arc 07), she goes underground seeking revenge against Il Collezionista's drow guild. May
  appear as an **NPC ally during the Notte dei Drow** at Rethmar, attacking the drow from within.

**Status (Current)**: Active. Il Collezionista escaped. Sonjak's forces operational. The party
missed the connection between the fungi experiments and the drow leadership during their
Underdark journey.

### Githyanki Dragon-Rider Faction

**Alignment**: LE
**Forces**: Githyanki warriors mounted on red dragons
**Goals**: Steal powerful artifacts from the mortal plane; the party's accumulated artifacts
(especially the sage monk's artifact at Dauth) have drawn their attention
**Campaign Role**: Interrupt Tordek's tournament at Dauth with a devastating aerial assault
**Connections**: Allied with the Red Hand horde's dragon contingent if not defeated at the tournament
**Status (Current)**: Approaching. Will strike during the tournament arc (09, P2B).

---

### Circle of Eight Mages (Circolo degli Otto)

**Alignment**: LN/NG
**Composition**: 8 powerful arcane casters
**Role**: Create a **mythal-like barrier** during the Battle of Rethmar to deflect enemy
spells and revive ancient stone statues within the city (similar to the Battle of Hogwarts
animated statues defending the castle)
**Narrative Purpose**: Provide the large-scale magical defense that allows the PCs to focus
on personal combat against enemy commanders rather than being overwhelmed by horde magic
**Status**: Arrive at Rethmar before the final battle. Active during arc 09, P3 phases.

---

### Hammerfist Dwarven Kingdom

**Alignment**: LG
**Location**: Hammerfist Holds (The North Wall mountains)
**Leader**: The Hammerfist King (quest-giver for the Corona di Adamantio quest)
**Role**: Commissioned the Rumbling Stone party to find the Corona di Adamantio based on
oracle/cleric visions of Moradin. Provided base of operations for Underdark descent.
**Military**: Dwarven army that fights alongside the party at the Battle of Hammerfist (arc 08)
and sends reinforcements to the Battle of Rethmar (arc 09) — fuse with city militia
**Connections**: Central to arcs 01–08; source of divine guidance through Moradin
**Status**: Damaged but standing after Battle of Hammerfist. Sending aid to Rethmar.

---

### Druid Circle of Nature

**Alignment**: N
**Role**: If Hella completes her Sacred Forest ritual challenges (arc 09, P1 — Cerchio Sacro
della Foresta), the druid circle offers their nature power for the Battle of Rethmar:
- Invocation of animals (summons)
- Shape-shifting transformations (like Beorn in The Hobbit)
- Temporary nature avatar forms for allied combatants
**Condition**: Hella must pass the ritual. If she fails, the circle remains neutral.
**Status**: Awaiting Hella's ritual completion.
